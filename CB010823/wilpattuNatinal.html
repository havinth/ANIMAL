Willpattu !!!


The biggest and one of the oldest national parks in Sri Lanka is Wilpattu National Park, which is situated in the northwest of the island. Wilpattu, a region well-known for its biodiversity and natural beauty, is made up of a variety of habitats, such as broad grassy plains, dense scrublands, and picturesque lakes, which offer a variety of settings for a variety of wildlife.

for more info

Wildlife Diversity:



Wilpattu's remarkable population of Sri Lankan leopards (Panthera pardus kotiya) is one of its main draws. Other creatures found in the park include crocodiles, water buffalo, elephants, sloth bears, and spotted deer. The park is a birdwatcher's paradise due to the abundance of lakes strewn throughout it, which enhances its wildlife.


Safari Experience:



Safari trips are available at Wilpattu, where guests can explore the park's extensive system of paths and tracks. The broad area and varied ecosystems of the park provide a singular and engaging safari experience. Seeing animals is an interesting journey because of the diverse landscape and plants.


Landscape and Lakes:



Wilpattu is known for its "villus," which are natural lakes surrounded by dense vegetation. These villus serve as important watering holes for the wildlife, creating picturesque landscapes and enhancing the chances of wildlife sightings.
